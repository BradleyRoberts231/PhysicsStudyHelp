Problem 1: The Speeding Cyclist

Problem: A cyclist accelerates from rest at a constant rate of 2 m/s¬≤ for 6 seconds. How far does the cyclist travel in that time?

  1. Understand the scenario: The cyclist starts from rest (\( v_0 = 0 \)).
  2. Knowns: \( a = 2 \text{ m/s}^2 \), \( t = 6 \text{ s} \)
  3. Use: \( \Delta x = v_0 t + \frac{1}{2} a t^2 \)
  4. Calculation: \( \Delta x = 0 + 0.5 \cdot 2 \cdot 36 = 36 \text{ m} \)
  5. Conclusion: The cyclist covers 36 meters in 6 seconds.

Problem 2: Falling Apple

Problem: An apple falls from a tree and hits the ground in 1.2 seconds. How far did it fall (ignoring air resistance)?

  1. Knowns: \( v_0 = 0 \), \( a = 9.8 \text{ m/s}^2 \), \( t = 1.2 \text{ s} \)
  2. Use: \( \Delta x = \frac{1}{2} a t^2 \)
  3. Calculation: \( \Delta x = 0.5 \cdot 9.8 \cdot 1.44 = 7.06 \text{ m} \)
  4. Conclusion: The apple fell 7.06 meters.

Problem 3: Braking Car

Problem: A car moving at 20 m/s stops in 4 seconds. What is the acceleration?

  1. Knowns: \( v_0 = 20 \text{ m/s} \), \( v = 0 \), \( t = 4 \text{ s} \)
  2. Use: \( a = \frac{v - v_0}{t} = \frac{-20}{4} = -5 \text{ m/s}^2 \)
  3. Conclusion: The car decelerates at 5 m/s¬≤.

Problem 4: Soccer Ball Displacement

Problem: A soccer ball is kicked at 8 m/s and accelerates at 1.5 m/s¬≤ for 3 seconds. What is its displacement?

  1. Knowns: \( v_0 = 8 \), \( a = 1.5 \), \( t = 3 \)
  2. Use: \( \Delta x = v_0 t + \frac{1}{2} a t^2 \)
  3. Calculation: \( \Delta x = 24 + 6.75 = 30.75 \text{ m} \)
  4. Conclusion: The ball traveled 30.75 meters.

Problem 5: Sprinter's Final Velocity

Problem: A sprinter starts from rest and accelerates at 3.2 m/s¬≤ for 5 seconds. What is their final velocity?

  1. Knowns: \( v_0 = 0 \), \( a = 3.2 \), \( t = 5 \)
  2. Use: \( v = v_0 + at = 0 + 3.2 \cdot 5 = 16 \text{ m/s} \)
  3. Conclusion: The sprinter reaches 16 m/s.
