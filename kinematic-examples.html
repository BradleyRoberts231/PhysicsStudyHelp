Kinematic Word Problems

These examples walk through kinematic problems step-by-step with full LaTeX formatting and clear physics explanations.

Example 1: Acceleration from Rest

Problem: A car accelerates from rest at a rate of \(3\,\text{m/s}^2\) for \(5\,\text{s}\). What is its final velocity?

We use: \(v = v_0 + at\)

\(v = 0 + 3 \times 5 = 15\,\text{m/s}\)

Example 2: Displacement During Acceleration

Problem: An object starts from rest and accelerates at \(2\,\text{m/s}^2\) for \(4\,\text{s}\). How far does it travel?

Use: \(x = x_0 + v_0t + \frac{1}{2}at^2\)

\(x = 0 + 0 + \frac{1}{2}(2)(4)^2 = 16\,\text{m}\)
