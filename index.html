Kinematic Equations

  • \( v = v_0 + at \)
  • \( \Delta x = v_0t + \frac{1}{2}at^2 \)
  • \( v^2 = v_0^2 + 2a\Delta x \)

Derivations

\( a = \frac{v - v_0}{t} \Rightarrow v = v_0 + at \)

\( \Delta x = v_0t + \frac{1}{2}at^2 \)

\( v^2 = v_0^2 + 2a\Delta x \)

Example Problems

A car accelerates from rest at 3 m/s¬≤ for 5 seconds: \( v = 0 + 3 \times 5 = 15 \, \text{m/s} \)

A rock falls for 4 seconds: \( \Delta x = \frac{1}{2}(9.8)(4^2) = 78.4 \, \text{m} \)

A train stops from 20 m/s in 100 m: \( v^2 = v_0^2 + 2a\Delta x \Rightarrow 0 = 400 + 2a(-100) \Rightarrow a = 2 \, \text{m/s}^2 \)
