Kinematic Equations

Basic Equations

  • \( v = v_0 + at \)
  • \( \Delta x = v_0t + \frac{1}{2}at^2 \)
  • \( v^2 = v_0^2 + 2a\Delta x \)

Derivations

1. \( v = v_0 + at \)

From the definition of acceleration:

\( a = \frac{v - v_0}{t} \Rightarrow v = v_0 + at \)

2. \( \Delta x = v_0t + \frac{1}{2}at^2 \)

Average velocity: \( v_{\text{avg}} = \frac{v_0 + v}{2} \)

Displacement: \( \Delta x = v_{\text{avg}} \cdot t = \frac{v_0 + v}{2} \cdot t \)

Substitute \( v = v_0 + at \) ‚Üí

\( \Delta x = \left(v_0 + \frac{1}{2}at\right)t = v_0t + \frac{1}{2}at^2 \)

3. \( v^2 = v_0^2 + 2a\Delta x \)

Using the previous equations and eliminating time:

\( v^2 = v_0^2 + 2a\Delta x \)

Example Problems

Example 1

A car starts from rest and accelerates at 3 m/s¬≤ for 5 seconds. How fast is it going?

\( v = 0 + (3)(5) = 15 \, \text{m/s} \)

Example 2

A rock is dropped off a cliff. How far does it fall in 4 seconds?

\( \Delta x = \frac{1}{2}(9.8)(4^2) = 78.4 \, \text{m} \)

Example 3

A train moving at 20 m/s brakes to a stop in 100 meters. What is its acceleration?

\( 0 = 400 + 2a(-100) \Rightarrow a = 2 \, \text{m/s}^2 \)
