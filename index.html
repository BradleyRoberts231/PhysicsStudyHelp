Example Problem: Kinetic Energy

Problem: A 1200 kg car is moving at a speed of 25 m/s. What is its kinetic energy?

Step-by-step solution:

  1. Understand the formula: The formula for kinetic energy is \( KE = \frac{1}{2}mv^2 \), where \( m \) is mass and \( v \) is velocity.
  2. Plug in known values:
    \( KE = \frac{1}{2} \times 1200 \times (25)^2 \)
  3. Calculate the square of the velocity:
    \( 25^2 = 625 \)
  4. Multiply:
    \( KE = 0.5 \times 1200 \times 625 = 600 \times 625 = 375000 \)
  5. Final Answer:
    The kinetic energy is \( 375,000 \) joules.
